07

GTM Priorities: Next 90 Days

The goal is not to run a broad campaign. It is to win the right 5-10 enterprise accounts in the next two quarters. Everything below is sequenced to maximize the probability of that outcome.

  • Map and activate the network before the outreach starts
    Provide Vieu with the leadership team LinkedIn CSV, Target Account List, and Introducer List within the first 7 days post kick-off. The quality of relationship mapping directly determines the quality of warm paths. This is the foundation everything else is built on.
  • Prioritize KSA first: the SAMA reference is a genuine door-opener
    SAMA-aligned delivery is a rare credential in the KSA market. Lead with it actively when approaching SAMA-regulated banks, insurance firms, and financial services enterprises. This is the clearest competitive differentiation from global SIs who lack deep KSA regulatory experience.
  • Segment outreach by product, not by generic "cloud services"
    Three separate entry conversations: AlgoI Outpost for regulated-sector CIOs and CISOs; AlgoData Vault for CDOs and analytics leads; Amrny for operational quick wins. Each has a different buyer and a different hook. Mixing them produces noise. Lead with one problem per conversation.
  • Leverage Redington's regional network actively, not passively
    Redington has existing enterprise relationships across the Gulf. Work Vieu's stakeholder maps against Redington's known accounts to identify overlap. These are the highest-probability warm path combinations. Don't wait for Redington to refer; bring them into specific account conversations.
  • Build a repeatable account-entry playbook from the first 5 wins
    Document what worked in the first wave of warm introductions: which persona responded, which product hook opened the conversation, which relationship pattern led to the meeting. This becomes the repeatable playbook for the sales team to operate independently of Oussama's direct involvement.

The foundation is already built before the kick-off call. Vieu surfaces early connection signals across the target account list and the team can begin fine-tuning based on initial feedback. In most cases there is at least one warm path ready to act on in week one itself.

Together, we review the prioritization of the target list, surface look-alike accounts worth adding, and walk through sample account plans to build confidence in the intelligence quality. Actionable leads continue to surface throughout this week, not after it.

Intro orchestration and account planning at scale
By week three we are ready to programmatize around the graph. Intro orchestration begins in earnest, account plans are live across the priority list, and the team has a repeatable process for working each warm path through to a conversation.

The engagement shifts from onboarding to full value delivery. The focus is ensuring every surfaced lead is actively worked and converting into qualified opportunities. Weekly rhythm tracks lead status, conversation progress, and any accounts needing a new angle or path.

Supporting active plays while building programmatic leverage
With plays running across the pipeline, the focus expands to finding more programmatic ways to leverage the platform across sales, marketing, and revenue operations. The goal is a motion that compounds beyond the engagement window, not one that stops when the contract does.
